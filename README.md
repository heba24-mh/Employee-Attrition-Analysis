📊 Project Description: Employee Attrition Analysis
This project analyzes the key factors influencing employee attrition using HR data. The goal is to identify patterns behind why employees leave and provide actionable recommendations for companies to improve retention.

🔍 Analysis Focus
Age vs Attrition: Younger employees show slightly higher attrition rates.

Salary vs Attrition: Employees with lower monthly income are more likely to leave.

Job Satisfaction vs Attrition: Low satisfaction strongly correlates with higher attrition.

Distance From Home vs Attrition: Employees living farther from the workplace tend to leave more often.

💡 Key Findings
Job satisfaction and salary are the strongest predictors of attrition.

Distance from home also plays a role, especially when combined with low satisfaction.

Younger employees may require more engagement and career development opportunities.

🛠️ Recommendations for CompaniesImprove job satisfaction through training, career growth, and better communication.

Review salary structures to ensure competitiveness and fairness.

Offer flexible work arrangements (remote work, flexible hours) to reduce the impact of distance.

Develop targeted programs for younger employees to increase engagement and retention.
